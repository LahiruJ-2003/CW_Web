2023 CAR F1 W14 E PERFORMANCE


Introducing the Mercedes-AMG F1 W14 E PERFORMANCE, the team's much-anticipated 2023 contender, born from the lessons learned during a challenging 2022 season. Building upon the foundation laid by its predecessor, the W13, the W14 has undergone targeted development to enhance its core performance aspects while retaining the distinctive DNA that makes it unmistakably Mercedes. Visually, the W14 boasts striking features, including the eye-catching gulley bodywork on the engine cover, which immediately captures attention. Yet, the team's commitment to improvement extends beyond appearances. A significant project aimed at reducing overall weight has resulted in a refined and evolved version of the iconic black livery that graced the car during the 2020 and 2021 seasons. Behind the wheel of this formidable machine are the celebrated duo of Lewis Hamilton and George Russell, who join forces for their second season together, ready to take on the fierce competition. Adding to the team's depth and talent is the promising presence of Mick Schumacher, serving as the third driver and offering valuable support to the dynamic duo. Team Principal, Toto Wolff, remains optimistic about their ambitions to compete for the World Championship title, but he acknowledges the strength of their competitors and the need to play catch-up. The Mercedes-AMG F1 W14 E PERFORMANCE symbolizes the team's relentless pursuit of excellence and their unwavering determination to achieve greatness on the Formula 1 stage.

A Refined Concept


"Toto reflects on the challenges faced last year and the valuable lessons they brought. He expresses his hope that 2023 will showcase the team's improved understanding in resolving issues and enhancing the car's performance. The W13 car exhibited impressive potential, but unlocking its full capabilities and effectively utilizing its downforce proved to be a hurdle. Though it showed great improvement towards the end of the season, the notorious bouncing issue at certain circuits persisted, and the drivers struggled to receive adequate feedback, limiting their ability to push the car to its limits. Undaunted, the teams at Brackley and Brixworth tackled the challenge head-on. They worked tirelessly throughout the year, addressing problems and refining the car's performance during the season. Their efforts culminated in a remarkable one-two finish at the SÃ£o Paulo Grand Prix, a testament to their progress. Several key areas were targeted for improvement. The team focused on reducing the overall weight of the car, ensuring a more consistent car balance across different speeds, and aligning the aerodynamic characteristics with the regulations. This led to significant changes, including a lighter chassis, revamped front suspension geometry, adjustments to the cooling system, and a refined aerodynamic concept built on the knowledge gained from the previous year. With a refined and optimized car, the team eagerly anticipates a successful and competitive 2023 season."

All in Performance


The team has made a bold decision to prioritize performance and has returned to a sleek black livery for the W14 car. Struggling to meet the weight limit posed a significant challenge for this generation of cars, prompting the team to draw inspiration from the legendary original Silver Arrows. However, instead of uncovering shiny aluminum like the Silver Arrows of the past, the W14 will showcase raw carbon fiber alongside matte black-painted parts. Toto explained that the team faced weight issues in the previous year, and for this season, they have meticulously worked to shave off every possible gram. The decision to embrace the black livery also holds historical significance. In 2020, the team changed its livery to support diversity and equality causes that hold a special place in their hearts. The color black became an integral part of their identity from that point forward, making their return to it a meaningful and purposeful choice. Moreover, the black livery carries echoes of the 1993 Sauber C12, a car powered by 'Concept by Mercedes.' This marked Mercedes' return to the pinnacle of motorsport after an absence since 1955, adding another layer of historical connection to their current decision. With a focus on performance and a nod to their heritage, the team eagerly anticipates an exciting and competitive season with the W14.

Incoming freeze and reliability fixes


Hywel Thomas, the Managing Director of Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains (HPP), revealed the team's focus on preparing for the upcoming season's final performance software freeze. This marks the last chance for development within the current regulation cycle, and it has driven the team to put in significant effort. Recent performance improvements have been achieved through optimizing the engine's software, which has become the primary area of advancement. The team understands the importance of maximizing this opportunity and has been working relentlessly to incorporate as many upgrades as possible. Collaborating as a cohesive unit, they have thoroughly examined design changes made to both the chassis and the engine, ensuring a comprehensive approach to their development. Emphasizing reliability, the team has prioritized the implementation of changes to the Power Unit to enhance its robustness, especially when dealing with potential issues of the car hitting the ground. With a dedicated and unified effort, Mercedes AMG HPP is gearing up for the challenges of the upcoming season, aiming to achieve peak performance while ensuring the reliability and durability of their Power Unit.
